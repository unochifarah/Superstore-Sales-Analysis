## Overview

The Superstore Sales Dataset is a retail dataset from a global superstore spanning 4 years, consisting of 18 columns and 9,800 rows covering orders, customers, products, and regional sales data.

---

## Questions Explored

1. Which state has the highest and lowest sales?
2. Which item is ordered the most?
3. Which item generates the highest revenue?
4. Which segment orders the most, and which generates the highest revenue?

---

## Process

For each question, I started by identifying which columns were relevant before building a pivot table and sorting the results. I also followed up on findings that raised new questions, particularly around the Home Office segment.

---

## Findings

- **California dominates sales** it has the highest total sales across all segments, and remains the top state even when filtered to Home Office customers specifically
- **Volume and revenue tell different stories** the most ordered item is Staple Envelope with 47 orders, but the highest revenue item is the Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier at $61,599.82 from just 5 orders
- **Home Office punches above its weight** despite having the fewest orders of the 3 segments, Home Office customers have the highest average spend per order, more than Consumer and Corporate

### Home Office Drill-Down
- Preferred shipping method: Standard Class (1,046 orders), least used: Same Day (112 orders)
- Top state: California with 325 orders
- Most ordered item: Easy-staple Paper (12 orders)

---

## Conclusion

Although the Consumer segment dominates in order volume and total revenue, the Home Office segment is worth closer attention, its customers spend more per order on average. The company could benefit from focusing retention and marketing efforts on Home Office customers, particularly in California, and ensuring Standard Class shipping remains reliable since it's the segment's clear preference.
